Chichen Itza Pyramid, Mexico

Chichen Itza is one of the most famous archaeological sites in Mexico, located in the Yucatán Peninsula. The centerpiece is El Castillo (Temple of Kukulcán), a massive step pyramid built by the Maya civilization around the 9th-12th centuries. The pyramid is renowned for its astronomical significance, especially during the spring and autumn equinoxes when a shadow forms the illusion of a serpent descending the steps. Chichen Itza was a major center for politics, religion, and trade, and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

  • Built by the Maya civilization between the 9th and 12th centuries
  • El Castillo pyramid is 30 meters (98 ft) high with 365 steps
  • Equinox phenomenon: "Serpent of light" appears on the pyramid
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site and New Seven Wonders of the World

Pioneering Archaeologists

Désiré Charnay

A French explorer and photographer, Charnay was one of the first to document Chichen Itza in the 19th century. His photographs and maps brought international attention to the site and inspired further research.

Edward Herbert Thompson

An American archaeologist, Thompson purchased Chichen Itza in 1894 and conducted extensive excavations, including dredging the Sacred Cenote. His discoveries of artifacts and human remains revealed the site's ritual significance.

Sylvanus G. Morley

A leading Maya scholar, Morley directed the Carnegie Institution's excavations at Chichen Itza in the 1920s and 1930s. He helped restore major structures and advanced the study of Maya hieroglyphs and history.

Alfred Tozzer

An American archaeologist and ethnographer, Tozzer contributed to early research and publications on Chichen Itza and the Maya civilization, shaping modern understanding of the site's cultural context.

Interesting Facts

  • Equinox Serpent: During the spring and autumn equinoxes, the pyramid casts a shadow that looks like a serpent slithering down the steps.
  • 365 Steps: El Castillo has 365 steps, one for each day of the year, reflecting the Maya calendar.
  • Acoustic Marvel: Clapping at the base of the pyramid produces an echo that resembles the chirp of a sacred quetzal bird.
  • UNESCO & Wonder: Chichen Itza is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
  • Astronomical Precision: The pyramid is aligned to mark solstices and equinoxes, showcasing the Maya's advanced knowledge of astronomy.
  • Ball Court: The site contains the largest ancient ball court in the Americas.
